Projected Income

Live and projected earnings for the shift, priced from your TBWU at the current rate schedule.

What it does

RADCounter turns your TBWU into dollars using your current rate and shows both earned-so-far and a projected end-of-shift figure. It's the same math as the productivity readouts, expressed in the units you actually care about.

How to turn it on / set it up

Set your rate under Settings → Compensation → Current $/TBWU rate. The projection uses the current rate schedule rather than a trailing average, so a mid-shift rate change is reflected immediately.

Using it

The compensation readouts sit alongside the RVU/TBWU metrics. The projection extrapolates from your current pace, so like Est Total it's most reliable once the shift is underway.

Tips & gotchas

  • Compensation is priced per clock-hour in Central time, so studies signed in different rate hours are valued at that hour's rate — this is exactly the effect the [[clario-compare-view]] delta isolates.
  • Projected income is an estimate for your own planning, not a payroll figure.
