MosaicTools Bridge

RADCounter's primary link to what you're reading — a named-pipe connection to MosaicTools that reports the current study and sign/discard events.

What it does

When MosaicTools is running, RADCounter talks to it over a named pipe to learn the current study, its details, and the moment you sign or discard — the fastest and most reliable way for RADCounter to know what you're doing.

Using it

The connection is automatic whenever both apps are running. The bottom-bar MosaicTools light is green while the bridge is live (see [[liveness-lights]]). Studies complete the instant a sign event arrives, which is what makes Recent Studies update immediately.

Tips & gotchas

  • If MosaicTools isn't running, RADCounter can fall back to reading Mosaic directly — see [[mosaic-cdp]].
  • Sign and discard signals are de-duplicated and cross-checked so a study is counted exactly once and a discard is never counted.
