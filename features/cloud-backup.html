Cloud Backup

Optionally back up your records to OneDrive or Dropbox so a shift's history survives a machine change.

What it does

RADCounter can back up its local records to your own OneDrive or Dropbox, so your shift history isn't tied to a single machine. It's opt-in and uses your own cloud account.

How to turn it on / set it up

Under Settings → Backup, check Enable OneDrive backup and/or Enable Dropbox backup and point it at your folder.

Using it

Once enabled, backups happen in the background. If you move to a new machine, restoring from the backup brings your history with you.

Tips & gotchas

  • Backups carry the same privacy posture as the local database: accessions are hashed and no patient information is included ([[accession-privacy]]).
  • It's your cloud account, not a RADCounter server — nothing is uploaded anywhere you don't control.
