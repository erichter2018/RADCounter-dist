Clario Work-Unit Comparison

Each study shows RADCounter's TBWU in blue with Clario's own per-study work unit in white directly underneath.

What it does

RADCounter reads Clario's own per-study work unit directly and shows it next to its computed TBWU, so you can see study by study where the two agree and where they diverge. In Recent Studies, RADCounter's TBWU appears in blue with Clario's number in white underneath, aligned.

How to turn it on / set it up

Turn on Settings → CDP → Enable Clario CDP (work-unit comparison), run the one-click Set up Clario CDP, and launch Clario from the shortcut it creates. Off by default. See [[cdp-setup]] for the full setup.

Using it

Clario's number lands a minute or so after you sign, once it finalizes on Clario's side, and slots in under the study. Nothing you see changes your counts.

Tips & gotchas

  • The comparison never changes your totals. Every RVU/TBWU/compensation figure stays computed exactly as before — Clario's number is display-and-compare only, with RADCounter's own value always the failsafe.
  • To compare totals over a whole shift or date range, use [[clario-compare-view]].
