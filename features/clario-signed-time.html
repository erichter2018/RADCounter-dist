Official Signed Time

RADCounter records Clario's official signed date and time for each study, used to compare compensation against the exact rate hour.

What it does

Along with the per-study work unit, Clario reports the official signed date and time for a study. RADCounter captures and stores that timestamp so compensation can be compared against the exact clock-hour rate a study was signed in.

Using it

The signed time is read alongside the work unit a minute or so after you sign, and is stored on the study's record. In the [[clario-compare-view]], it's the timestamp on Clario's side of the compensation delta — your locally detected completion time versus Clario's official signed time.

Tips & gotchas

  • Clario's timestamp is Central time; RADCounter normalizes it correctly when pricing against the per-hour rate schedule.
  • Today this is used for comparison, keeping RADCounter's own detected time as the working figure. It's the groundwork for optionally making Clario's signed time authoritative later.
